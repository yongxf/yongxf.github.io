Learning Grasp Exploration for Robust Bin Picking by RCNN-ISF


Yongxiang Fan*, Hsien-Chung Lin*, Te Tang*

Welcome!

This page supplements our CS280 Project submission report, in which we present an approach to plan robust grasps for bin picking with customized grippers by a novel approach that combines iterative surface fitting (ISF) and region with convolutional neural network (R-CNN).

Compared with general parallel grippers, the customized grippers have specifically designed fingers to increase the contact area with the workpieces and improve the grasp robustness. However, grasp planning for customized grippers is challenging due to the object variations, surface contacts and structural constraints of the grippers. In this project, an iterative surface fitting (ISF) algorithm is proposed to plan robust grasps by fitting the finger shapes towards the object surface. ISF simultaneously searches for optimal gripper transformation and finger displacement by minimizing the surface fitting error. A learning based grasp exploration method using region with convolutional neural networks (R-CNN) is introduced to avoid ISF getting stuck in bad local optima and to improve the collision avoidance performance. The proposed RCNN-ISF is able to consider the structural constraints of the gripper, learn grasp exploration strategy from previous experience, and plan optimal grasps in clutter environment in real-time. The effectiveness of the algorithm is verified by both simulations and experiments.

Heavy Clutter Environment

As the point cloud getting more complicated, the ISF initialization by K-means clustering becomes less-efficient. We utilize R-CNN to learn the good regions to initilize ISF search.

Failure Mode
